# Member Engagement & Dining Trends Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes member behavior and dining activity at a private social club, **The Quin House** in Boston.
The goal is to uncover patterns in member engagement, peak usage times, and dining trends to support better operational and business decisions.

---

## Objectives

* Identify peak hours and busiest days for member check-ins
* Analyze monthly engagement trends
* Understand member behavior before and after dining
* Highlight highly engaged vs. low-engagement members
* Provide insights to improve staffing, operations, and member experience

---

## Dataset

The data used in this project includes:

* Member check-in data (PeopleVine)
* Food & beverage transaction data (Toast POS)

*Note: Data has been anonymized to protect member privacy.*

---

## Key Insights

* Peak check-in hours occur during late afternoon and evening periods
* Certain days consistently show higher member engagement
* A small percentage of members contribute to a large portion of total visits
* Opportunities exist to re-engage low-activity members
